Business Analyst


Release Planning

The objective of release planning is to estimate which features will be delivered by the release deadline (fixed release deadline), or to choose a rough delivery date for a given set of features (fixed scope). As a Business Analyst, I assist Product Owner to list out all the high level features (Epics) at the top row to kickstart the release planning. Then I will work with Development Team to list out all the user stories under the respective Epic, in the order of dependency from top to bottom.

Following I will list out Sprint number at the left side and gather everyone to to further arrange the user stories with respect to the Sprint number. Product Owner will determine what to be delivered for each Sprint while Development Team will assess on the feasibility and dependency. Finally, the release planning is completed and every month I will assist Product Owner to conduct release planning with user feedback from Sprint Review to update the features and schedule continuously, to deliver the product of high business value.


Writing User Story

As a Business Analyst in an Agile team, it is crucial to write user story in the user perspectives, so that Product Owner can prioritize the user stories, while Development Team will not over-engineer and focus on the business value for the implementation.

Furthermore, I also adopted Behaviour Driven Development (BDD) to write acceptance criteria, whereby the acceptance criteria is specified by describing how its behaviour should appear to an outside observer. This allow Development Team to understand the behaviour easily with clarity as well as using it to write automated tests, while Product Owner can use this as a test script for acceptance of the user stories.

In this way, the user story is the single source of truth for Product Owner, Scrum Master and Development Team, enabling effective communication and better collaboration, thereby delivering a high quality product.



Business Process Modelling

As a Business Analyst in a large organisation, I am dealing with users or clients with complex workflow in their day to day operation, so I use Business Process Modelling (BPM) to model out the workflow for better analysis and communication with Product Owner, Scrum Master and Development Team.

To model the workflow, I will work closely with Product Owner to conduct Product Conceptualisation sessions with the users. This involves the users mapping out their current workflow and indicating what is their wishlists and painpoints at the various stages of the workflow. With this information, I will work out workflows using BPM to satisfy most the concerns and work iteratively with the users for feedback for enhancement.

Scrum Master


Agile Coaching

Scrum is a simple framework to understand but it is not easy to execute. Many Scrum teams failed as they uses a lot of workarounds called Scrumbuts in the hope to optimize the process without a clear understanding of Scrum. As a Scrum Master, my role is to ensure that the team have the Agile maturity to run Scrum effectively by coaching them to understand and apply Agile principles and practices diligently.

For Development Team, I coach them on Agile Engineering Best Practices, including Test Driven Driven (TDD) and Continuous Integration (CI) and work closely with the Technical Lead to pair the junior developer to the senior developer for practice.

For Product Owner, I coach them on how to prioritize the Product Backlog, which is the key to develop product that deliver high business value. In addition, I educate Product Owner how to write good user stories so that information is communicated effectively to Development Team for implementation.


Facilitation

Scrum is one of the most structure methodology of Agile with several key Sprint Ceremonies, namely Daily Standup, Sprint Planning, Product Backlog Refinement, Sprint Retrospective and Sprint Review, to shorten feedback loop. As a Scrum Master, I conduct these ceremonies at the start of the project to bring in structure to the development. During each the ceremonies, I coach Product Owner and Development team on why and how to run the ceremonies effectively and also optimize the ceremonies over time to suit the nature of the team.

One of the main concern of growing Agile teams is that Scrum Master may become the bottleneck if the team is always dependent on Scrum Master. So as part of the Agile adoption process, I will gradually take a back seat and let the team take over the ceremonies as the team grow in Agile maturity. In this way, the team will collaborate directly and become independent with ownership, allowing them to become truly self-organized.



Problem Solving

The ultimate goal of any product development projects is to deliver the product effectively, so as a Scrum Master I help the team to perform at their highest level by removing any impediments to progress. To identify impediments, I conduct Sprint Retrospective to collect feedback from Development Team. One of the technique I used is called "Glad, Sad, Mad", where the team will talk about their emotions through the Sprint. From the negative emotions, I will identify the problems that caused them which are usually the impediments.

Impediments can be anything, so I have to be creative and flexible to solve them. For example, it can be that Product Backlog is not ready, so I will work closely with Product Owner to refine the backlog. Or it can be two of the team members are not communicating due to personal issues, so I will become the mediator and counsellor between them to resolve their conflicts to get them talking to each other again.
